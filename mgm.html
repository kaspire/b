— Pattern Statistics · Triggers vs Wins —
1-2-X · single → pair, bet chop—
A single then a pair of the other side (B PP / P BB). Bet the chop back — BPP→B, PBB→P — 1 bet. Win = the pair stays a pair (doesn't extend to 3+).
2-1-1 & 1-1 · chop → expected streak
Bet the chop (opposite of the last outcome) and watch the new streak. 2-1-1 (BB-P-B / PP-B-P) is the stronger signal; 1-1 (any two differing) fires far more often. Histograms show how long the streak ran — miss = chop never started.

1-1-3-X · the favorite (2 bets)—
1-1 then a 3-streak (BPBBB / PBPPP). Bet the chop on the 4th & 5th outcomes, stop on first win. Win = the streak breaks by the 5th; miss = it reaches 5 in a row.
After 4 in a row—
Every time a side hits 4 straight — does it extend to 5+ or stop at 4? Read it as continuation (extend) or reversal (stop), whichever way you'd bet it.
